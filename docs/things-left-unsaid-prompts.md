# Things Left Unsaid — Image Generation Prompts

Things Left Unsaid is a visual companion to the music.

The collection is built around evidence of a life rather than portraits
of a person.

The recurring theme is a single warm light against a great deal of
darkness.

These images are not album covers, promotional photography, or artist
portraits. They are quiet observations — traces of someone's presence,
moments that feel interrupted rather than staged. The goal is not
beauty or drama. The goal is memory.

This document is a prompt reference only. No images have been generated
from it, and no assets or site changes are included in this commit.

## Shared style block

Prepend to every prompt below (or set as a persistent style reference if
your tool supports it):

> Realistic photography, shot on a 35mm full-frame camera, 50mm lens,
> shallow depth of field, natural and practical lighting only. Color
> grade: near-black shadows (#080807–#181815), warm amber porch-light
> glow (#c87941–#d9894e), faded cream highlights (#d5caa8) catching on
> weathered wood and worn surfaces. Quiet rural Midwest setting, late
> evening or night. Slight film grain, no digital sharpening, no HDR.
> The scene feels paused mid-moment, like someone just stepped away.
> Documentary-style, unposed, unpretentious — not a postcard, not an
> advertisement.

## Universal negative prompt

Apply to all ten:

> No people, no human figures or silhouettes, no faces or hands, no
> stage lights, no spotlights, no microphones or mic stands, no concert
> or performance setting, no cowboy hats, no text, no titles, no logos,
> no watermarks, no album-cover layout or composition, no dramatic
> cinematic color grading (no teal-orange, no lens flare, no light
> leaks), no fog machines or theatrical smoke, no fantasy or surreal
> elements, no oversaturation, no glamour lighting, nothing that looks
> staged or art-directed.

## Prompts

**1. Empty rocking chair on a porch at night**

> An empty wooden rocking chair on a sagging porch at night, mid-rock as
> if just vacated, slightly turned toward the porch steps. A single
> porch light overhead casts a warm amber pool across worn floorboards;
> everything past its reach falls into near-black shadow. Peeling
> paint, a faint sweater or flannel draped over one arm of the chair.
> Cricket-quiet stillness. Long exposure feel, slight motion blur on
> the chair to suggest it's still settling.

**2. Acoustic guitar leaning against a porch railing**

> A worn acoustic guitar leaning against a weathered wooden porch
> railing, strap hanging loose, a thin layer of evening dew on the
> wood. Warm amber porch light spills from just out of frame, catching
> the curve of the guitar body and the grain of the railing while the
> background dissolves into deep shadow. No case, no stand — left
> there, not displayed. Slightly off-center composition, like a quick
> glance rather than a portrait.

**3. Long gravel driveway with a porch light glowing in the distance**

> A long, straight gravel driveway stretching into near-total darkness,
> flanked by overgrown grass and a few bare trees, with a single small
> porch light glowing warm amber far in the distance at the end of the
> drive. Low camera angle, ground-level gravel texture sharp in the
> foreground, everything else softening into shadow. Foggy breath-cold
> night air, no moonlight exaggeration — just that one distant light
> doing all the work.

**4. Kitchen table with coffee mug and closed notebook**

> A plain wooden kitchen table at night, lit by a single warm overhead
> bulb or a lamp just out of frame. A half-full coffee mug with a faint
> ring of condensation, a closed spiral notebook with a pen resting on
> top, maybe a chair pushed back at an angle. Worn tablecloth or bare
> wood grain. Amber light pooling on the surface, edges of the room
> fading to charcoal black. Feels mid-thought, interrupted.

**5. Rain falling through warm porch light**

> Rain falling steadily through the cone of light cast by a single
> porch lamp at night, each raindrop catching a brief amber glint as it
> passes through the beam. Wet wooden porch boards reflecting the light
> in streaks. Background is soft black, blurred by the rain itself.
> Long shutter speed to show rain as fine streaks, not frozen droplets.
> No lightning, no storm drama — just steady, quiet rain.

**6. Window glowing from inside a dark house**

> A single window glowing warm amber from inside an otherwise dark
> house at night, viewed from outside across a dim yard. Curtain
> half-drawn, glass slightly fogged at the edges. The house's siding
> and roofline are barely visible, swallowed in charcoal shadow except
> where the window light touches the wall around it. No visible figure
> inside — just the light and a hint of a lampshade or ceiling.
> Stillness, like the house is holding its breath.

**7. Mailbox at dusk with a lit home far behind it**

> A weathered metal mailbox on a wooden post at the edge of a gravel
> road at dusk, slightly tilted, flag down. Behind it, far down a dirt
> path, a small house with one porch light glowing amber against a
> deep blue-black sky. Wide shot, mailbox sharp in the foreground, the
> house small and soft-focused in the distance. Dusk gradient from
> charcoal sky to near-black tree line. Quiet, end-of-day stillness.

**8. Porch swing gently moving at night**

> A wooden porch swing on chains, caught mid-sway as if someone just
> stood up from it, on a dark porch lit by a single warm bulb. Faint
> motion blur on the swing's chains and seat to suggest movement
> without showing who caused it. Worn cushion with a slight
> indentation. Amber light catching the chain links and the edge of
> the porch post; the yard beyond dissolves into black. Cool night air
> implied by visible breath-fog or dew, not by anything dramatic.

**9. Tire tracks in fresh snow leading toward a lit house**

> Fresh, untouched snow except for a single set of tire tracks curving
> up a driveway toward a small house with one porch light glowing warm
> amber in the distance. Night sky deep charcoal-black, snow lit faintly
> blue-white in the open but warming to amber near the house. Bare
> trees lining the drive, snow undisturbed elsewhere — no footprints,
> no second set of tracks. Cold, hushed, recently-passed-through
> feeling.

**10. Guitar case beside a front door**

> A worn, hard-shell guitar case set down beside a front door on a dim
> porch, latch undone, leaning slightly against the doorframe as if
> just carried in. A single porch light overhead casts warm amber light
> across the case's scuffed corners and stickers, with the doorway
> itself dark and the house number barely legible in shadow. Welcome
> mat slightly askew. No guitar visible, no instrument — just the case,
> like the night isn't over yet.
